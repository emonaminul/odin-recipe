Egg Curry

INGREDIENTS:

  1. Yield
  2. 4 servings
  3. ¼ cup neutral oil, such as grapeseed or canola
  4. 2 medium yellow onions, chopped
  5. 6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  6. 1(2-inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and finely chopped (about 2 tablespoons)
  7. 2 small cinnamon sticks
  8. 6 green cardamom pods
  9. 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  10. ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  11. ½ teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  12. 6 Roma tomatoes, finely chopped
  13. 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  14. ½ teaspoon garam masala
  15. 6 to 8hard-boiled eggs, peeled
  16. Handful of small, tender fresh cilantro stems

PREPARATION:

In a large, heavy skillet, heat the oil over medium. Add the onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until evenly caramelized and light brown, 25 to 30 minutes. Stir in the garlic and ginger, and fry, stirring occasionally, another 4 minutes. Add the cinnamon and cardamom. When the spices start to toast, after about 2 minutes, stir in the coriander, turmeric and peppercorns. Add the tomatoes, salt and 1 cup water. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens and the fat rises to the top, about 15 minutes. Stir in the garam masala and lower the heat. If the sauce isn’t runny, stir in ½ cup water. Add all but 2 of the eggs and stir gently. Halve the remaining eggs lengthwise and arrange on top, yolk-side up. Heat until the eggs are warmed through, then top with cilantro.
